## Content Principles

### 1. French as Editorial Source of Truth
- **French (FR)** is the primary editorial language
- **English (EN)** and **Arabic (AR)** must be equivalent in meaning, not literal word-for-word translations
- All content should be reviewed for meaning equivalence across locales

### 2. Tone
- **Calm**: Reassuring, not alarmist
- **Helpful**: Action-oriented, practical
- **Simple**: No jargon, accessible language
- **Short sentences**: One idea per sentence
- **Action-first**: Start with verbs, be direct

### 3. Page Structure Requirements
Every page MUST have:
- ✅ **Clear H1**: Main heading that describes the page
- ✅ **1-2 line "why it matters"**: Brief explanation of importance
- ✅ **1 primary CTA**: Main call-to-action button

## Tone Examples

### ✅ Good (FR)
> "Le NAS te relie aux impôts, à l'emploi et aux programmes fédéraux. Sans lui, aucun employeur ne peut te payer légalement."

**Why it works:**
- Short, clear sentences
- Action-oriented ("te relie", "peut te payer")
- Direct consequence stated

### ❌ Bad (FR)
> "Le Numéro d'Assurance Sociale (NAS) constitue un identifiant unique délivré par Service Canada qui permet de lier votre profil fiscal à votre statut d'emploi et d'accès aux programmes fédéraux, et sans lequel aucun employeur ne pourra procéder à une rémunération légale."

**Why it doesn't work:**
- Too long, complex sentence
- Passive voice ("constitue", "permet de")
- Jargon-heavy ("identifiant unique", "profil fiscal")

### ✅ Good (EN - Canadian English)
> "Your SIN links you to taxes, employment, and federal programs. Without it, no employer can legally pay you."

**Why it works:**
- Natural Canadian English (not British or American)
- Equivalent meaning to FR
- Short, direct

### ✅ Good (AR - Modern Standard Arabic)
> "يربطك رقم التأمين الاجتماعي بالضرائب والوظيفة والبرامج الفيدرالية. بدونه، لا يمكن لأي صاحب عمل أن يدفع لك بشكل قانوني."

**Why it works:**
- Modern Standard Arabic (not dialect)
- Neutral, professional tone
- Equivalent meaning to FR/EN

## Microcopy Guidelines

### CTAs (Call-to-Actions)

**Primary CTAs:**
- `Commencer` / `Start` / `ابدأ` - Start journey/checklist
- `Voir la checklist` / `View checklist` / `عرض قائمة التحقق` - View checklist
- `Voir le parcours` / `View journey` / `عرض الرحلة` - View journey
- `Voir les guides` / `View guides` / `عرض الأدلة` - View guides
- `Trouver un bureau` / `Find an office` / `العثور على مكتب` - Find office location

**Secondary CTAs:**
- `Continuer` / `Continue` / `متابعة` - Continue to next step
- `Télécharger` / `Download` / `تحميل` - Download PDF/checklist
- `Partager` / `Share` / `مشاركة` - Share checklist
- `Réinitialiser` / `Reset` / `إعادة تعيين` - Reset progress

## Section Order Standard

All guide pages should follow this order:

1. **What** (Qu'est-ce que c'est ?)
2. **Why** (Pourquoi c'est important ?)
3. **How** (Comment faire)
4. **Docs** (Documents nécessaires) - if applicable
5. **Avoid** (À éviter)
6. **Smart Tips** (Bon à savoir) - if applicable
7. **Sources** (Sources officielles)

**Exception:** Scam guides use custom structure:
1. Scenario
2. Red Flags
3. Actions
4. Never Do
5. Mantra
6. Sources

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Don't:
- Use jargon ("identifiant unique", "profil fiscal")
- Write long, complex sentences
- Use passive voice when active is clearer
- Mix formal/informal tone
- Literal word-for-word translation
- Use dialect in Arabic
- Hardcode text in components
- Skip the "why it matters" line
- Forget the primary CTA

### ✅ Do:
- Use simple, everyday language
- Write short, clear sentences
- Use active voice
- Maintain consistent tone
- Translate meaning, not words
- Use Modern Standard Arabic
- Use shared content layer
- Include "why it matters"
- Always have a primary CTA

---

## Content Review Process

1. **Write in French first** (source of truth)
2. **Review for tone** (calm, helpful, simple)
3. **Translate to EN** (Canadian English, meaning-based)
4. **Translate to AR** (Modern Standard Arabic, meaning-based)
5. **Verify equivalence** (all three mean the same thing)
6. **Check vocabulary** (use glossary terms)
7. **Verify structure** (H1, intro, CTA, sections)
8. **Test RTL** (Arabic spacing and alignment)
